## Headline

Farmers and agents see the grants landscape as too complex and fragmented since
BPS was phased out, distrust the RPA/Defra, and many can't apply without an
agent's help — with consistent pain across discovering, applying for and claiming
grants, and a real risk of digital exclusion for "analogue by default" farmers.

## Context: jobs to be done

Against macro pressures (low margins, high input costs and debt, climate, the
post-Brexit shift from BPS to many targeted grants), the research frames four
farmer "jobs to be done":

1. **Run a viable business** — *HMW ensure farmers receive grant funds as quickly
   and efficiently as possible?*
2. **Anticipate and manage risks** — *HMW help farmers plan ahead, make good
   financial decisions, and understand the grants landscape?*
3. **Navigate regulatory & environmental challenges** — *HMW make grants and
   environmental policy easier to understand and access in the context of a
   farmer's own operation?*
4. **Ensure long-term viability of the farm** — *HMW help farmers feel certain,
   optimistic and in control of long-term viability?*

## Themes

1. **Macro pressures shape how farmers consume grants.** Global-market and
   supply-chain pressure, high input costs, high debt, low cash ("asset rich, cash
   poor"), climate impacts and frequent grant changes mean farmers seek funding
   simply to stay viable.
2. **Grant schemes feel too complex — and increasingly so.** The shift from BPS
   ("simple, one form, one payment a year") to many unfamiliar, changing schemes
   requiring separate applications for much the same information. *"It seems
   bonkers to be applying for productivity and animal health and welfare
   separately when it's a lot of the same information."*
3. **The grants & payments landscape is complicated.** Too many schemes; similar,
   confusing, unmemorable names; overlapping criteria; complex and inconsistent
   application requirements between schemes.
4. **Many farmers can't apply without an agent.** Complexity drives reliance on
   agents — *"90% of the time we are doing the application for them"* — who are
   both frustrated by and commercially reliant on that complexity.
5. **Low trust — RPA/Defra seen as disconnected and high-risk.** Requirements feel
   too prescriptive and not feasible; a perception that the RPA looks for fault and
   withholds money. *"Any engagement with the RPA is a risk… the highest risk on my
   risk register."*
6. **Farming is localised and contextual.** Schemes are felt to ignore the local,
   contextual detail farmers (as custodians) know best; participants favour a more
   collaborative, less prescriptive approach.
7. **A significant group are "analogue by default" — risk of digital exclusion.**
   Paper records, low digital skills, limited hardware (scanners, smartphones) and
   help. *"A lot of the farmers I know will not be able to do this… they won't know
   how to digitise the paperwork."*
8. **Informal, localised supply chains.** Second-hand kit, preferred local
   suppliers, trusted help, and the recycling of BPS money — flexible purchasing
   requirements are needed.
9. **Perception that grants favour larger, intensive operators.** Larger farms can
   afford consultants, in-house research and upfront capital; smaller operators
   feel disadvantaged. *"Grants are designed for people who don't really need the
   money, not small holders who are struggling."*
10. **Consistent pain across the journey — discover, decide, apply, claim.** Hard
    to find and understand schemes; eligibility/scoring confusing; repeated
    information; not all journeys start in the familiar RPA portal; restrictive
    windows out of sync with the farming calendar; item specs/codes confusing; the
    "3 quotes" rule hard for specialist items; payment delays and cashflow strain;
    no visibility of claim status; all-or-nothing claims. *"Can't track claim as no
    statuses are reported with FETF, so I have no idea where the claim is."*

## Improvement directions (as raised in the deck)

**Process:** review/simplify guidance · make evidence easier to submit (digital and
offline) · start all applications from the RPA portal · reuse applicant
information and data Government already holds (Companies House, HMRC) · reduce
claim-processing time · give better status updates.

**Grants as products:** simplify and standardise scheme rules · simplify
eligibility/scoring/assessment · align application windows with the agricultural
calendar and review their length · review evidential requirements · provide a
fuller forward look · review the quantity and names of grants and schemes.

## Why it matters for caseworking

This is applicant/agent context, but it directly shapes the caseworker system: the
complexity, repeated information, restrictive windows and lack of claim-status
visibility farmers feel are the other side of the manual, fragmented processing
RPA caseworkers describe (see *Future Grants — RPA internal user problems*, Aug
2024). The screen-level FETF apply/claims studies referenced here test designs
that respond to these themes.
