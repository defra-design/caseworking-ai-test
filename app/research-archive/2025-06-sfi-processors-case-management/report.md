## Headline

Extending the RPA workload survey to **SFI Processors (n=76 combined)** confirms the
same priorities hold across teams. Staff most want **everything about a case in one
place (88%)** and end-to-end progress tracking. The strongest case-list filters are
**SBI (87%), Case status (78%) and Application reference (72%)** — a clear steer for
caselist search and columns, and evidence a single case-management design can serve
both FTF Appraisers and SFI Processors.

## Why this report exists

The FTF appraiser debrief flagged concurrent research with SFI staff "to ensure
future case-management designs are aligned and usable across both Appraisers and
SFI teams." This deck reports that combined picture: the same survey, now answered
by **76 respondents** spanning FTF Appraisers and SFI Processors.

## Most valuable features (combined survey, n=76, pick 3)

| Feature | % |
|---|---|
| Having all information in one place | **88%** |
| Track progress from application to payment | 45% |
| See full history of messages and actions | 43% |
| Direct messaging with applicants | 25% |
| Clear deadlines and reminders | 22% |
| Be able to assign or reassign a case | 12% |

"Everything in one place" dominates across both teams — reinforcing the
single-case-view direction.

## Most helpful filters (combined survey, n=76, pick 5)

| Filter | % |
|---|---|
| SBI | **87%** |
| Case status (e.g. in progress, on hold) | **78%** |
| Application reference | **72%** |
| Who it is assigned to | 46% |
| Applicant name | 46% |
| Submission date | 40% |
| Business name | 38% |
| Due date | 28% |
| Applicant location | 4% |

The combined data sharpens the top filters: **SBI, case status and application
reference** are the clear priorities, followed by assignee and applicant name.

## Shared findings (see companion report)

The frustrations (manual processes & lack of automation; unfair, opaque allocation;
process delays & bottlenecks; complex, scattered guidance; poor customer comms),
the desired solutions, the search-first behaviour (project reference → SBI →
business name), the rich status-label vocabulary, and the distinct Appraiser vs
Workflow-Manager needs are all carried in the
[10 June appraiser debrief](../2025-06-rpa-appraiser-research/report.md). The
workflow-process rating (3.35/5, n=37) is unchanged here.

## Next steps (from the deck)

Align case-management designs across FTF Appraisers and SFI teams; agree, design and
build the **MVP**, targeting a **working private beta by September 2025**; continue
partnering with the Appraisers team over the summer to test and refine.

## Why it matters for caseworking

This is the strongest single steer in the archive for **caselist search and
filtering**: SBI, case status and application reference are the filters staff want
most, and "everything in one place" is the dominant feature ask — both validated
across two teams. It directly informs the live caselist's columns, filters and
status terminology, and confirms the single case-view direction taken into the MVP
that the Jan 2026 amendments study later tests.
