## Headline

RPA caseworkers process FTF and FETF grants through Giles plus a sprawl of Excel
and Word files. Most applications fail first time, and manual checking,
fragmented guidance and poor system integration drive delays and errors — the
as-is problem set the caseworking prototype is meant to solve.

## Background

The Future Grants team's objectives are to source a substitute for **Giles**,
help bring grants to market faster, and improve the experience for applicants and
RPA staff. Building on the 2021 RPA Discovery Report, this study deepens the
picture of the current process for handling **Improving Farm Productivity (FTF)**
and **Farming Equipment & Technology Fund (FETF)** applications. It is the
"internal user problems" output (a companion "description of the FTF/FETF process"
was produced separately).

## Method

7 × 1-hour depth interviews (23 Jul – 5 Aug 2024) with RPA staff across appraiser,
SME and management grades. The team notes the study is a **partial view** (not all
roles covered) and that findings are interpretative.

## Key problems

1. **Most grant applications fail first time.** Staff estimate 90%+ of FTF and
   ~60% of FETF applications need further correspondence — missing documents,
   wrong formats, missing/incorrect invoices, missing match-funding evidence, not
   registered with RPA, missing permissions, FETF items not matching the
   category spec. *"90+% of applications have to go back."* / *"100% of [FTF]
   applications need to be revised."*
2. **Applicants don't understand what's required.** They don't know preferred
   formats, that they must be registered, how permissions work, what evidence is
   acceptable, or how much detail to give. *"Sometimes they'll write War and Peace
   … and sometimes they'll be very brief."*
3. **Application forms are too complicated.** Known to be complex/confusing;
   much technical content is set by other bodies (e.g. the Environment Agency) and
   defended as "as complex as it needs to be."
4. **Most of the work is done in Excel and Word.** Cases are logged in Giles, but
   active checks and scoring run in Excel/Word on SharePoint (ECR, scoring
   spreadsheet, tracker) with heavy copy-paste and duplication. *"Once it's in
   Giles, it gets pulled out and put on … actually 3 spreadsheets."*
5. **Manual processes need time-consuming manual checking.** Laborious checking
   and re-checking at multiple stages, across many documents and guidance. *"As
   part of the business and supplier checks, we have to take screenshots of
   everything."* Manual steps also cause "small human errors."
6. **Systems are inflexible to different digital formats.** Incompatible
   supporting-information formats cause delays; e.g. third-party transfer links
   (WeTransfer) can't be accepted.
7. **Internal guidance can be overwhelming.** Guidance is spread across many
   places (RPA hub, Word, Excel, SharePoint folders); staff keep several guidance
   pages open at once.
8. **RPA staff cover multiple schemes.** Non-specialists need a working knowledge
   of many schemes; *"it can be difficult to stay on top of it all."*
9. **Lack of integration with RPA and other organisations.** Many checks are
   manual — RPA call centre, registration/permissions, Companies House,
   dual-funding checks with other arm's-length bodies; disconnected CRM.
10. **The 'clock stop' mechanism may incentivise delays.** Appraisers have 60
    (FTF) / 30 (FETF) working days; stopping the clock for missing info resets the
    KPI and may be used to manage busy periods.
11. **Technical checks cause significant delays.** Slurry applications need
    Environment Agency checks, typically 2–9+ months.
12. **Application windows + seasonality cause volume spikes.** Tight windows and
    the farming calendar create spikes that are hard to staff.
13. **Concerns about agent-submitted quality.** Most FTF/FETF applications come via
    agents; staff perceive these as poorer quality and sometimes unresponsive.

## Other issues

- Claims require a **wet signature**.
- Checking company accounts requires **specialist knowledge**.
- FETF **item categories** confuse both applicants and staff.
- RPA staff have **limited visibility of the whole application process**.

## Reflections on Giles

Staff say Giles "rarely causes problems, owing to its lack of functionality" — it
stores case information, supports the clock stop, saves correspondence and helps
calculate FETF payments. Its problems: you can't see applicant/advisor
permissions, it's hard to find things, and it doesn't remind you to do things.
*"GILES doesn't do a huge amount. It just stores information … done manually most
of the time."*

## "How might we…" prompts raised

Get applications right first time · make requirements clearer · design forms
applicants can complete unaided · cut manual processes · reduce processing errors
· increase confidence in completeness/accuracy · accommodate varied formats ·
reduce reliance on guidance · ease scheme-recall pressure · better integrate
systems · reduce clock stops · speed up ALB checks · smooth demand spikes ·
support better agent submissions · allow digital signatures.

## Why it matters for caseworking

This is the **as-is baseline** for the caseworker prototype: the move off
Giles/Excel/Word toward a single system that reduces manual checking, consolidates
guidance, surfaces permissions, and tracks case progress. Later studies in this
archive test the prototype screens that respond to these problems.
