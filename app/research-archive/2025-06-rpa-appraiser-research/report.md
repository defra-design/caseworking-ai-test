## Headline

FTF appraisers and workflow managers rate current workload processes **3.35/5**.
Their top frustrations are manual trackers/spreadsheets, unfair and opaque work
allocation, QC/panel bottlenecks, scattered guidance and poor customer comms. They
most want **applicants to upload evidence directly (68%)**, **automated third-party
checks (46%)** and **in-system messaging (43%)**. The team is moving to build an
MVP case-management system, targeting a **private beta by September 2025**.

## Why we ran this

To ground future case-management improvements in the real, day-to-day needs of FTF
operational staff: understand pain points across the grant lifecycle, explore what
changes would help most, and gather feedback on the early **case list** and **case
view** concepts — how staff search for and access project information, how they
organise and prioritise work, and what features a redesigned system needs.

## How staff rate things today

Asked how well current workload-management processes work (1–5), the **average was
3.35/5** (n=37).

## Top frustrations (survey, open responses)

1. **Manual processes and lack of automation** *(top theme)* — heavy reliance on
   trackers, spreadsheets and manual checks. *"Zero automation, no case management
   function, reliant on trackers to support."* / *"Way too many spreadsheets and
   systems to update for each individual project."*
2. **Work allocation & prioritisation issues** — poor workload balancing, no
   transparency, fairness concerns. *"Work is allocated to you even if you have a
   lot of cases already."* / *"KPIs are not an accurate representation because of
   regular RFIs dropping priority status."*
3. **Process delays & bottlenecks** — long waits for QCs, SMEx advice and panel
   decisions; KPI clock resets seen as gaming the system. *"It takes far too long
   to get a QC back."*
4. **Complex, unclear or burdensome guidance** — hard to navigate, easy to miss
   points. *"The fact that very few applications go through without an RFI is clear
   evidence that the process is too difficult."*
5. **Communication with clients** — constant back-and-forth email, blank Word/Excel
   documents that invite errors, an outdated email-inbox interface. *"Interface
   between customer/RPA too outdated (email inboxes being relied upon)."*

## What staff would change (desired solutions)

- **Automation & reducing manual work** — automate key tasks, integrate trackers
  into one system, reduce duplication. *"Take away the need for manual
  intervention."*
- **Process simplification & streamlining** — *"It's too stoppy and starty. One
  process where you do everything and get it QC'd at the end."*
- **Guidance clarity & usability** — coherent, scheme-specific guidance structured
  around how staff actually work; in place before new schemes launch.
- **Customer-facing improvements** — applicant document upload, pre-filled business
  information, clearer prompts; KPIs that measure something in the customer's favour.
- **Work allocation & management visibility** — give managers visibility of case
  complexity and team workload; allow fairer, FTE-based bulk allocation.

## Most valuable features in a new system (survey, pick 3)

| Feature | % |
|---|---|
| Applicants can upload evidence directly | **68%** |
| Automated 3rd-party checks | 46% |
| Direct messaging with applicants | 43% |
| See full history of messages | 43% |
| Clear deadlines and reminders | 32% |
| Track progress from application through to payments | 30% |
| Be able to assign or reassign a case | 16% |

## How staff find a case

When searching for a project, staff reach first for the **Project reference
number** (the single most common term, in nearly every response), then **SBI**
(often combined with project number), **business name** (when the project number
isn't known — common on applicant phone calls), and occasionally **applicant /
contact name** or **GILES number**.

**Most-helpful filters (survey, pick 5):** Project reference (89%), SBI (62%),
Project Appraiser (62%), Name of grant (57%), KPI status (49%), Case status (46%),
Business name (38%), Applicant name (30%), Project title (22%), Submission date
(8%), Applicant location (3%), Policy area (0%).

## Status labels staff want

Staff described a rich status vocabulary across the lifecycle — useful input for
case-list status terminology:

- **Initial:** Unassigned · To be allocated · Application received · Initial checks
  in progress
- **In progress:** ECR in progress · Full appraisal in progress · Awaiting internal
  referral (policy/compliance) · Awaiting external referral (fraud) · Technical /
  accounts review
- **On hold / waiting:** Awaiting customer info (RFI) · Awaiting SMEx advice · On
  clock stop · Rework required (returned for amendment)
- **Quality checks:** Awaiting ECR QC · ECR QC in progress · Awaiting FA QC · FA QC
  in progress
- **Decision & closure:** Approved · Contracted · Rejected · Withdrawn · Closed
- **Tracking / indicators:** KPI status (Low/Med/High) · Case owner · Linked cases
  (same customer) · Custom note / reminder

## Summary of needs

**Appraisers need to:** find and return to active projects easily; see urgent
actions and deadlines clearly; know when a customer has responded; have a simple,
accurate workload overview; reduce manual data entry and duplicate checks; view
progress and interactions in one place; access clear guidance/checklists;
confidently search for case details; and work on fairly allocated cases.

**Workflow Managers need to:** see what each team member is working on; track all
active projects; filter and plan by scheme type and deadlines; understand case
complexity for fair allocation; minimise manual workarounds; monitor cases on hold
or awaiting customer action; keep guidance current; and see customer progress and
outstanding actions.

## Why it matters for caseworking

This validates and quantifies the Jan 2025 case-management study (see *Usability
testing of RPA case management designs*) and sets the requirements for the MVP that
the later *Amendments to grant applications pre-agreement* study (Jan 2026) tests.
Several priorities here map directly onto live caselist work — status terminology,
search by reference/SBI/business, fair allocation and assignment, and a single
case view — while applicant upload, automated checks and in-system messaging remain
the headline outstanding asks.
