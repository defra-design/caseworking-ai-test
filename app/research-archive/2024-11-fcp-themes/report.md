## Headline

Farmers and advisors experience the grants space as hard to navigate — analogue
by default, low trust in Defra/RPA, easily-confused schemes, and overwhelming
guidance. These discovery themes are the backdrop the caseworking system is being
designed within; the team flagged a deeper debrief to follow.

## Context

An early **discovery** readout exploring the general challenges in the grants
space. The deck frames it as "Next, we want to explore some of the general
challenges in the grants space" and closes "We will explore these ideas in more
depth in an upcoming debrief…". It is applicant-/advisor-facing context rather
than a usability test of the caseworker prototype, so it captures **no caseworking
screens** — but it sets the problem space those screens sit within.

## Themes

1. **Many farming processes are analogue by default.** Smaller operators say
   their processes are paper-based and they would struggle to digitise them.
2. **Low trust in Defra and RPA among farmers and advisors.** Participants feel
   Defra and RPA are difficult to work with and detached from the realities of
   farming.
3. **It is easy to confuse different schemes.** Participants frequently struggle
   to differentiate between schemes.
4. **Grant benefit skews towards larger operators.** Many feel the complexity of
   grant schemes favours larger, more intensive operators.
5. **Applying for grants feels onerous.** Many farmers say the proposed process
   feels more onerous than previous processes.
6. **It is difficult to understand eligibility and scoring.** Profitable farms
   rely on agents to decipher the rules; smaller operators can feel left behind.
7. **Grant guidance is overwhelming.** Participants feel existing guidance is
   verbose and difficult to understand.
8. **Grant application windows make planning difficult.** Timelines are tight and
   the forward look is limited.

## Why it matters for caseworking

These themes describe the applicants and advisors whose grant applications
caseworkers process. Low trust, scheme confusion and an onerous, verbose process
on the applicant side translate into the cases, amendments and queries caseworkers
handle — context for the later, screen-level studies in this archive.
