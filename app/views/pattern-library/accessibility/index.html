Accessibility

Services built with this library must meet WCAG 2.2 AA. GOV.UK and MOJ components are accessible out of the box — most risk in this prototype comes from the hand-built custom styles. This page covers what to check.

Focus & keyboard

  • Every interactive element shows the GOV.UK focus state — see Focus & interaction states.
  • The custom quiet secondary button (.gem-c-button--secondary-quiet) and the filter tags were styled by hand — confirm they keep a visible focus state and are reachable by keyboard.
  • Sortable table headers and the filter must be fully keyboard operable, with state announced (aria-sort, aria-expanded).
  • Hit targets are at least 44 × 44px.

Colour & contrast

  • Never use colour as the only signal — status is always carried by tag text too.
  • Check the custom tints against their background for 4.5:1 (normal text):
    • Not accepted — $cw-tag-red-text on $cw-tag-red-bg.
    • Action required — $cw-tag-action-text on $cw-tag-action-bg.
    • The changed-value highlight ($cw-tag-changed-bg) — ensure text over it still passes, and add a non-colour indicator.
  • Record the measured ratios here once checked, so the result is auditable.

Forms & errors

  • Group related inputs (decision radios, date inputs) in a <fieldset> with a <legend>.
  • On failure, show an error summary, move focus to it, link each item to its field, and prefix the page title with "Error:".
  • Inline error messages use aria-describedby and a visually-hidden "Error:".

Known patterns & workarounds

  • Right-aligned tag in a details summary — the :has() override in application.scss removes a propagated underline so it doesn't paint through the tag. Keep this documented so it isn't "tidied" away.
  • MOJ ports — because MOJ Frontend isn't installed, ported components must keep their ARIA. When porting JS-driven ones (button menu, multi-file upload), bring the behaviour, not just the look.

Before you ship a screen

  1. Tab through the whole page — can you reach and operate everything?
  2. Zoom to 200% — does anything break or overlap?
  3. Run a screen reader over the key flow.
  4. Check contrast on any custom colour you introduced.
  5. Confirm errors are announced and linked.
